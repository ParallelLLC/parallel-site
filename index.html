Vector

Network and influence intelligence from sparse public signal

A dual-use capability for mapping the actors, communities, and pathways that move a market or a mission. Vector reconstructs influence and community structure from limited public data, identifies the nodes that matter, and surfaces the routes to reach them, with privacy-preserving and responsible-use controls built in from the ground up.

Proven in enterprise deployment. Architected for regulated and mission-critical environments.

Most influence and network analysis breaks in exactly the conditions where the answer matters most: when the data is thin, the environment is adversarial, and the output has to withstand review. Follower counts and engagement dashboards describe what is loud, not what is load-bearing. Vector is built for the harder problem, identifying the structurally important actors and communities in a segment from incomplete signal, and doing it in a way that survives an audit of how the conclusion was reached.

What it does
  • Identifies who matters, not who is loud. Ranks the actors and communities that genuinely drive demand or carry influence in a target segment, so teams concentrate effort on the nodes that move outcomes rather than the ones with the largest surface metrics.
  • Maps the pathways, not just the targets. Surfaces the communities worth owning and the routes into them, so reach is built through network structure rather than disconnected outreach.
  • Operates on the data you already control. Works with first-party data and public signal, fits the existing stack rather than replacing it, and is designed to perform where data is scarce or degraded rather than assuming clean, abundant inputs.
  • Defensible by construction. Built-in privacy-preserving and responsible-use controls, with brand-safety and integrity constraints and explicit limits against impermissible targeting. Every ranking is traceable, so the reasoning behind an output holds up under review.

Built for environments with dynamic constraints

Vector is designed to the same standard as the rest of what we run: observable, recoverable, and documented so the system outlasts any one operator. Decisions are recorded, outputs are auditable, and the architecture assumes scrutiny and scale from day one rather than retrofitting them later. The enterprise deployment proves the engine works in the field. The architecture is built so the same capability transfers into regulated and mission-critical settings where the cost of being wrong, or of being unable to explain why, is high.

Deployed with a multi-state client operating across five states spanning the South, Mid-Atlantic, Midwest, and West Coast, and actively expanding.
